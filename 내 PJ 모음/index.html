Lady Hideko

“A 25-year-old Japanese noblewoman who inherited a huge fortune from her parents. Despite her outstanding beauty and enormous wealth, she is naive and does not know men well.

The only daily routine was to take a walk in the back garden near the mansion or to practice reading books to be read at a reading session regularly hosted by her uncle in the study. She lives like a bird trapped in a cage, and is about to be married to an aunt who covets her property.

Sook-Hee

A 17-year-old girl chosen by the Count who needed a role to shake off Hideko Izumi. She lives a hard life with the money she earned while working as a pickpocket. She was born with a good personality with a strong motherhood. She made a deal with the Count for Hideko's property, thus entered the Kouzuki mansion and received the Japanese name ‘Tamako’ and became a servant of Lady Hideko.

Count Fujiwara

His real name is Go Pan-dol and he is a Korean swindler who seeks Izumi Hideko's huge fortune. Pretending to be a Japanese count from Japan, he plans to marry Hideko and rob her of her fortune. He tries to seduce Hideko by entering her mansion under the pretext of Hideko's Western painting class.

Uncle Kouzuki

Hideko Izumi's uncle and guardian.

Although he is Korean, he admired Japan and continued to connect with the Governor-General of Korea as an interpreter, and he became very rich by receiving the right to mine gold mines with the help of the annexation of Korea. He married a daughter of a fallen noble family in Japan to become a Japanese to the core and changed her family name to Kozuki.

He needs Hideko's property, and thus tries to marry Hideko, his wife's nephew.
